# Course 5 – Assets, Threats, and Vulnerabilities

Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate

---

## Overview

This course introduced the core concepts of protecting organizational assets by identifying threats, vulnerabilities, and security risks. It focused on asset classification, common cyber threats, vulnerability management, and security frameworks used to strengthen an organization's security posture.

---

## Core Concepts Covered

* Security Assets
* Asset Classification
* Threats & Threat Actors
* Vulnerabilities
* Risk Assessment
* Common Cyber Attacks
* Security Controls
* Defense in Depth
* Security Frameworks
* The CIA Triad
* Risk Management
* Vulnerability Management

---

## Security Assets

Learned about the different types of assets that organizations must protect.

Topics included:

* Physical assets
* Digital assets
* Intellectual property
* Sensitive information
* Critical business resources

---

## Threats & Threat Actors

Studied the different types of threats and the individuals or groups responsible for cyber attacks.

Topics included:

* External attackers
* Insider threats
* Nation-state actors
* Cybercriminals
* Hacktivists

---

## Vulnerabilities

Explored how weaknesses in systems, applications, and processes can be exploited by attackers.

Topics included:

* Software vulnerabilities
* Misconfigurations
* Weak authentication
* Unpatched systems
* Human error

---

## Risk Assessment

Learned how organizations identify and evaluate security risks.

Topics included:

* Risk identification
* Risk analysis
* Likelihood and impact
* Risk prioritization
* Risk treatment strategies

---

## Security Controls

Studied different methods used to reduce security risks and protect organizational assets.

Topics included:

* Administrative controls
* Technical controls
* Physical controls
* Preventive controls
* Detective controls
* Corrective controls

---

## Security Frameworks

Introduced to widely used cybersecurity frameworks and best practices.

Frameworks covered:

* NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
* NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)
* CIS Critical Security Controls

---

## CIA Triad

Learned the three fundamental principles of information security.

Components:

* Confidentiality
* Integrity
* Availability

These principles serve as the foundation for designing secure systems and protecting organizational data.

This course strengthened understanding relevant to:

* Security Operations Analyst

The concepts learned provide a strong foundation for identifying security risks, protecting organizational assets, and implementing effective security controls.
